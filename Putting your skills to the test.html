Putting your skills to the test

There are challenges throughout this course. I’ve split them into three levels:

  • Mini-challenges
  • Skill-builders
  • Design Implementations

Mini-challenges

There will be a lot of mini-challenges. Not every lesson will have one, but most do, and many lessons have several of them sprinkled in. They are small challenges that might take only one line of code to solve. Some are a little more involved, but in general, they won’t take more than 60 seconds of your time to complete.

Some mini-challenges reinforce what we’ve just covered, and other mini-challenges will make you implement something new, but you will have enough information to figure it out.

Some of them will have hints if you are stuck, and almost all of them will have a solution, usually in video form.

Mini-challenge #1: Your favorite named color

It’s time for your very first mini-challenge! Change the color of the text to your favorite named color value! I’m quite partial to lightseagreen myself.

If you aren’t familiar with all the named colors, this is a great color picker.

Skill-builders

Skill-builders are the next step up from mini-challenges, in that they will have a bit more work to do, but should be solvable in 5-15 minutes.

These are meant to combine several concepts into one challenge and will sometimes involve things we haven’t fully covered in order to complete them. They aren’t meant to be easy to complete.

These will be accompanied by hints, and after all the hints, a solution video that goes into detail on both the problems and solutions.

They’ll generally use the built-in editor like the mini-challenge above, but they will be working with larger examples, so you’ll either want to work in full-screen mode, or bring the code into your editor and work locally.

Design implementations

Design Implementations are, as the name implies, where I will be giving you a design, and it’s your job to implement it. These will take longer, generally at least 15 minutes, and often quite a bit longer.

They will take everything you’ve learned so far, including from previous modules, and make you put it all into action.

For these, I will provide the designs and text, along with optional starting HTML & CSS. If you want to do the entire thing from scratch, that’s awesome, but the starting code will be there as a shortcut if you want.

Hints for these will be provided, but I won’t provide full solutions for most of them. The solutions to them are in the course material, and in some cases, I might provide links to external resources that could be helpful as well.

For the most part, there will be one of these per module.

Some challenges build on previous ones. There is a design implementation at the end of the next module, which we will then be revisiting for several challenges afterwards. I will be providing starting code for anyone who didn’t complete the challenge, but ideally, you’ll continue working from the first version you completed.
